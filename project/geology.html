Penghu Geology

Most of the Penghu Islands are composed of basalt. Basalt is a type of black rock comes from a volcano. After thousand years of weathering and erosion, basalt geology and landforms are everywhere in Penghu. Various carving of nature have amazed geologists and praised Penghu as "God's Stone Sculpture Park".

During the Japanese occupation, a wharf was planned to be built at the seaside of Daguoye to improve marine communications between Magong Island and Xiyu. While the workers were digging earth and stones away, they found the impressive columnar basalt which had been buried and had been in sound sleep for thousands of years. We can say that it is the most accessible columnar basalt in Magong Island.

Whale Cave was originally a basalt cape. However, after a long period of erosion by the northeast monsoon and seawater, a cape-shaped sea arch was eventually formed due to sea erosion. It looks like a beached whale, hence the name "Whale Cave".

Tongpan Island is surrounded by basalt stone pillars with well-developed joints. It is known as the "Yellowstone Park" of Penghu. The columnar joints and plate-shaped cracks on this island are very developed. Due to severe weathering and erosion, the tops of some basalt columns have been weathered into spherical shapes. It is a very special terrain landscape in Penghu.
