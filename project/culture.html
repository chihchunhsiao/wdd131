Penghu Culture

Due to the geographical situation, historical background and other special factors, the Penghu Islands have developed unique coral houses built with coral and basalt as building materials for a long time, forming the famous Erkan Historic Village, and retaining their local beliefs and food culture.

Because most of the residents in Penghu are engaged in fishing. Facing the impact of the ever-changing sea area and the harsh climate of Penghu, they rely on the gods to support their hearts. Thean Hou Temple is the most representative temple building of local beliefs.

Have you heard of Coral House?
Erkan Village is a historic settlement located on the Penghu Islands. The village is known for its unique architectural and cultural heritage. It's buildings were made by local materials, such as coral stones, and basalt.

In Penghu, the land is barren and the climate is harsh. Penghu cannot produce farm products, but the sea generously nourishes Penghu people with seasonal seafood. You can easily enjoy delicious seafood in Penghu restaurants.
